Renewable Insulation 

Name: Eshan Iyer

Class Period: 1A

Home Campus: Frisco High School

Group Members: Akshitha Chintalapati, Eshan Iyer, Brady Hicks

Project Objective:  Create an effective piece of insulation that is renewable, recyclable, and economical. Our goal is to trap the heat that is generated by a heat lamp inside a box.

Design Process: Construct piece insulation out of everyday materials. Heat the box without insulation for 20 minutes. Insert the temperature sensor into the box and place the insulation on top of the box (heating phase). Turn off the heat lamp inside and record through the heat sensor (cooling phase). Put the data on a spreadsheet, create a graph, and solve different thermodynamic equations.

Learning Target: Learn how to apply the basic principles of thermodynamics to a real-world scenario.

Personal Reflection: I learned the real-world applications of thermodynamic principles, how to make an effective insulation material, and be resourceful. Our group can improve on communication as, on the day of the experiment, we were not prepared to build our insulator. This led us to rely on others to source various materials that we did not bring. We were loaned items, which led to a very low build cost. I overall felt that I did my fair share in the group's effort in this project

Using this drawing, I used a utility knife to cut a provided 1-foot stick of balsa wood into segments. Next, I used glue to adhere these segments together. After letting the glue dry overnight, I cut out â€œgusset platesâ€ from a sheet of paper and glued them to the joints. After that dried, my truss was ready to test. To test the truss, I put it in the SSA machine. This machine would give me the maximum load the truss could withstand. 

Learning Target:  Learn further about trusses and the forces that each member experiences, such as internal and external forces.

Personal Reflection: I learned the applications of trusses and the importance of their design. It amazed me that one of the softest types of wood in the world could withstand over 40 pounds of force with proper design. In addition to learning about the actual applications of trusses and the importance of their design, I also gained a deeper understanding of the engineering principles behind them. I saw firsthand how even a tiny miscalculation or error in the design process could have significant consequences, highlighting the need for precision and attention. Overall, it was a rewarding experience to finally put all the theoretical knowledge I had learned into practice and see the results firsthand.
