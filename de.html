Q1: Random Number Generator

Name: Eshan Iyer

Period: 1A

Home Campus:  Frisco High School

What did you learn? I learned how to solder on circuit boards and implementation of the 555 timer. Also general understanding of diodes like LEDs, resistors, and capacitors. Many times I saw integrated circuits but never truely understanding how it worked. This activity helped me understand that.

What was the most challenging? The most challenging part of this activity was the soldering. Melting the right amount of sodder was the hardest part. I would melt too much and it would look blobby instead of a uniform rounded pyramid shape.

During this project me and my partner poorly organized our components and would get them mixed them up. I would organize the components into piles if I did this another time.

Q2: Majority Vote Project

Name: Eshan Iyer

Period: 1A

Home Campus:  Frisco High School

What did you learn? I learned how to properly implement the lessons about AOI (AND, OR, Inverter) logic into practice. I learned about putting logic into logic expressions and simplifying that with Boolean Algebra to get the most efficient circuit.  

What was the most challenging? The most challenging part of this project was implementing the logic in NAND and NOR formats. There was so many gates and at the end I had to simplify the double inversions which took some time. The reason there was so many gates for the Universal gate implementation is because each AOI gate splits into many Universal gates making it hard to keep track of the logic at times.

What would you do different? I would better organize myself, especially when doing the Universal Gate implementation. Keeping closer eye on the conversion while noting down the potential double inversions would've greatly reduced any potential confusion and frustration that the process would entail. 

Q3: Date of Birth

Name: Eshan Iyer

Period: 1A

Home Campus:  Frisco High School

What did you learn? Through this project, I learned about designing circuits for common-cathode seven-segment displays, including how to create truth tables, simplify logic expressions using Karnaugh maps, implement circuits using AOI gates, and simulate circuits using TinkerCAD. I also gained practical experience in implementing logic circuits using NAND and NOR gates.

What was the most challenging? The most challenging aspect of this project likely revolved around simplifying logic using Karnaugh maps. While the concept isn't overly complex, visualizing and optimizing logic expressions efficiently, particularly with multiple variables and expressions, proved challenging. Ensuring the circuits functioned as intended during simulation phases also presented hurdles, especially when addressing design or implementation errors.

What would you do different? In retrospect, to improve efficiency and effectiveness, I might consider breaking down the project into smaller, more manageable tasks with clearer milestones. This could help in better managing time and resources throughout the project lifecycle.

Q4: Digital Clock/Timer

Name: Eshan Iyer

Period: 1A

Home Campus:  Frisco High School

What did you learn? I learned how to implement a Hex decoder and synchronous circuits that depend on each other. This project also furthered my knowledge of J/K and D flip-flops and how they play into counters. It allowed me to apply by knowledge of the SSI and MSI implementations and the ranges that counters would go.

What was the most challenging? This project was overall very challenging but the hardest part was the interaction between the logic for each seven-segment display's logic. In this each segment increments based the on the value of another display and deciphering that interaction took a lot of time and effort.

What would you do different? I would understand the logic in a theoretical sense before trying to implement it into the circuit. With a greater understanding of the basics of how everything worked and interacted with each other would've made me to do the project more quickly and accurately.

Karnaugh Mapping

Name: Eshan Iyer

Period: 1A

Home Campus:  Frisco High School

What did you learn? This lesson taugh me how to K-Map, a way to more easily write logic expressions from truth tables. With a K-Map, I didn't need to do long Boolean Algebra to find simplified logic expressions anymore.

What was the most challenging? Understanding what values went where was the most challenging. It was hard to adjust to this method as it was more visual compared to Boolean Algebra. I had to visually analyze it instead of use rules and theorems to simplify logic expressions.

What would you do different? I would read the notes more carefully and fully understand this. I initally went into it with a surface level understanding instead of a good understanding. This made this activity difficult at first to do.

AOI Logic Design

Name: Eshan Iyer

Period: 1A

Home Campus:  Frisco High School

What did you learn? In this activity I learned one of the most basic skills in this class, the ability to write logic expressions. This lesson allowed me to visualize the outputs of a circuit in a way that I could simplify in the future using Boolean algebra or K-mapping. 

What was the most challenging? The most challenging was the conversion of the circuit to logic expression. I had trouble understanding the meaning of the gates when it came to the logic expressions and understanding Product of Sums Form.  

What would you do different? To improve in the future, I would dedicate more time to understanding the fundamental concepts of logic gates and their corresponding logic expressions. Additionally, I would practice more with examples and exercises to become more familiar with the process of converting circuits to logic expressions. Lastly, I would actively engage with my peers or seek assistance from my instructor to clarify any confusion or questions I may have encountered during the learning process.
